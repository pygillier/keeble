# How to Reboot Your Internet Router 🔌

If your internet is slow, keeps dropping, or simply stopped working, rebooting your router is the **first thing to try**. It fixes most common issues and takes less than 2 minutes.

---

## Before You Start

Make sure you know where your router is. It's usually a small box with blinking lights, placed near a window, in a hallway, or wherever your internet provider installed it. It may look like one of these:

- A small rectangular box with antennas sticking out
- A tall cylindrical device
- A flat disk (like a Google Nest or Eero)

> ⚠️ **Important:** Rebooting your router is safe and will not change any of your settings or passwords. It simply restarts the device, just like restarting your phone.

---

## What You'll Need

- ✅ Access to your router (no tools required)
- ✅ 2 minutes of patience

---

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Step 1 — Find Your Router

Locate your router at home. It is the box provided by your internet company (ISP), such as Orange, SFR, Free, Bouygues, etc. It will have several blinking or solid coloured lights on the front.

---

### Step 2 — Unplug the Power Cable

Look at the **back of the router** and find the power cable — it's the one going into a wall socket or a power strip.

👉 **Pull it out** from either the router end or the wall socket end. Either works.

> 💡 Don't press any buttons yet — just unplug the power.

---

### Step 3 — Wait 30 Seconds

This is the most important step that most people skip!

⏳ **Wait at least 30 seconds** before plugging it back in.

This gives the router enough time to fully shut down and clear its memory. Counting slowly to 30 in your head works perfectly.

---

### Step 4 — Plug It Back In

Plug the power cable back in, either into the wall or into the router — whichever end you unplugged.

You should see the lights on the front of the router turn on immediately.

---

### Step 5 — Wait for the Lights to Stabilise

This is where you need a little more patience. 🙏

The router needs **1 to 3 minutes** to fully restart and reconnect to your internet provider. During this time, the lights will blink rapidly — that's completely normal.

**Wait until the lights stop blinking and become steady.** Usually, a steady white or green light means everything is working.

> 🔴 If a light stays red or orange after 3 minutes, skip to the "Still Not Working?" section below.

---

### Step 6 — Test Your Connection

Once the lights are steady, try using the internet:

- On your phone or computer, open a browser (Safari, Chrome, Firefox…)
- Go to any website, like **google.com**
- If the page loads — **you're done! 🎉**

If you were connected via Wi-Fi and your device seems to have lost the network, simply:

1. Go to your device's **Wi-Fi settings**
2. Select your home network from the list
3. It should reconnect automatically

---

## Still Not Working?

If the internet is still not working after rebooting, try these extra steps:

### Check the cables
Make sure all the cables going into your router are firmly plugged in. Give each one a gentle push to make sure none have come loose.

### Reboot your device too
Sometimes the problem is your phone or computer, not the router. Try turning your device off and on again.

### Wait a bit longer
Occasionally your internet provider is experiencing an outage in your area. Wait 10–15 minutes and try again.

### Contact your internet provider
If nothing works after 15–20 minutes, call your internet provider's customer support line. They can check if there's a problem on their end and guide you through additional steps.

---

## Quick Summary

| Step | Action | Time |
|------|--------|------|
| 1 | Find your router | 1 min |
| 2 | Unplug the power cable | Instant |
| 3 | Wait | 30 sec |
| 4 | Plug back in | Instant |
| 5 | Wait for lights to stabilise | 1–3 min |
| 6 | Test your internet | 1 min |

---

> 💬 **Pro tip:** If you find yourself rebooting your router more than once a week, it may be time to contact your internet provider — your router might need to be replaced.
